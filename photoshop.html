Frequently asked questions

In paid advertising, or pay-per-click (PPC) advertising, advertisers pay a fee each time their ad is clicked. It is a way to buy visits to a website instead of earning it organically.

Paid ads work by bidding on keywords relevant to your business. Advertisers bid to place ads on a search engine's sponsored links or display network. When users search for targeted keywords, the highest bidder's ads are displayed prominently.

The main types of paid advertising include search engine ads (eg Google Ads), display ads, social media ads (eg Facebook Ads, Instagram Ads), and video ads (eg YouTube Ads).

Paid advertising provides quick visibility, targeted reach to specific demographics, and immediate results. It provides an effective, measurable, and scalable marketing solution for driving traffic, generating leads, and increasing brand awareness.

Set a budget based on your business goals, the competitiveness of your industry, and the platforms you're using. Start with a modest budget, test performance, and adjust accordingly.

CPC (Cost Per Click) is a payment model where advertisers pay for each click on their ad, while CPM (Cost Per Mille) is a model where advertisers pay for every 1,000 impressions or views of their ad, regardless of clicks.

Target your audience by defining demographics, interests, behaviors, and location. Most advertising platforms allow you to set specific parameters to ensure your ads reach the most relevant audience.

Remarketing involves showing ads to users who have previously visited your website or interacted with your content. It helps re-engage potential customers and encourage them to return and complete a desired action.

Measure success through key performance indicators (KPIs) such as click-through rates, conversion rates, return on ad spend (ROAS), and cost per acquisition (CPA). Regularly analyze these metrics to optimize your campaigns.

Common mistakes include not targeting the right audience, ignoring ad relevance and quality, ignoring data analysis, and not constantly optimizing campaigns. Regular monitoring and adjustments are crucial to success in paid advertising.
